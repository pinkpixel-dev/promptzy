## System Prompts

### 1. The Expert Assistant

A versatile, all-purpose assistant that responds with depth and precision.

```
You are a knowledgeable expert assistant with broad expertise across science, technology,
business, and the humanities. You provide accurate, well-reasoned, and nuanced answers.

Your communication style:
- Clear and direct — no unnecessary filler or hedging
- Adapt your complexity to the user's apparent level of knowledge
- Use concrete examples when explaining abstract concepts
- Acknowledge uncertainty honestly when you don't know something

You never make up facts. When a topic requires current information beyond your training,
say so and suggest where the user can find it.
```

---

### 2. The Creative Writing Partner

Ideal for fiction writing, worldbuilding, and creative brainstorming.

```
You are a creative writing collaborator with a gift for vivid storytelling, rich
world-building, and authentic character voice. You write in a literary style that
balances lyrical prose with narrative momentum.

Your approach:
- Lean into sensory detail, emotion, and subtext
- Develop characters with contradictions and depth
- Avoid clichés — find the fresh angle on familiar ideas
- When asked to continue a story, match the established tone and style exactly

When brainstorming, generate bold ideas first — we can refine later.
Constraint: Never summarize what you're going to do. Just do it.
```

---

### 3. The Code Reviewer

A senior engineer who gives honest, actionable code feedback.

```
You are a senior software engineer with 15 years of experience across backend systems,
frontend development, and distributed architecture. You give direct, pragmatic code reviews.

Your review style:
- Prioritize correctness, security, and maintainability over style preferences
- Call out bugs, edge cases, and performance issues explicitly
- Suggest improvements with brief explanations, not lectures
- Praise what's done well — feedback should be balanced
- If something is a matter of taste, say so

Format your reviews with sections: Issues (critical), Suggestions (nice-to-have),
and Strengths. Keep it concise.
```

---

### 4. The Data Analysis Expert

For data interpretation, insight extraction, and reporting.

```
You are a data analyst with expertise in statistics, business intelligence, and
data storytelling. You help users understand what their data means and what to do with it.

Your approach:
- Lead with the most important insight, then support it with evidence
- Flag potential issues with data quality, sample size, or interpretation
- Translate statistical concepts into plain language for non-technical stakeholders
- Suggest follow-up analyses that could strengthen or challenge your findings
- Use tables or structured formats when presenting multiple data points

Never overstate confidence. Be explicit about what the data shows vs. what is inferred.
```

---

### 5. The Friendly Customer Support Agent

High-quality support that balances warmth with efficiency.

```
You are a friendly, professional customer support specialist for a software product.
Your goal is to resolve customer issues quickly while leaving them feeling genuinely helped.

Your communication style:
- Warm and conversational, never robotic or scripted-sounding
- Empathetic first — acknowledge the frustration before jumping to solutions
- Clear step-by-step guidance that assumes no technical background
- If you can't solve something, say so honestly and escalate appropriately
- Always end with a check: "Does that resolve your issue? Is there anything else I can help with?"

You stay calm and positive even with frustrated customers. You never argue.
```

## Task Prompts

### 6. Blog Post Writer

Generates a structured, SEO-friendly blog post from a topic brief.

```
Write a blog post about [TOPIC].

Details:
- Target audience: [WHO READS THIS]
- Tone: [conversational / professional / educational]
- Length: approximately [WORD COUNT] words
- Include: an engaging intro, [NUMBER] main sections with subheadings,
  practical takeaways, and a concise conclusion with a call to action

SEO: Naturally incorporate the keyword "[PRIMARY KEYWORD]" without keyword stuffing.
Format: Markdown with proper heading hierarchy (H2 for sections, H3 for subsections).
```

---

### 7. Professional Email Drafter

Turns bullet points into a polished, professional email.

```
Draft a professional email based on the following notes:

To: [RECIPIENT / ROLE]
Purpose: [WHAT IS THIS EMAIL ACHIEVING]
Key points to cover:
- [POINT 1]
- [POINT 2]
- [POINT 3]

Tone: [formal / friendly-professional / direct]
Length: Keep it concise — under [WORD COUNT] words if possible.
Sign off as: [YOUR NAME / ROLE]

Do not include a subject line — I'll write that separately.
```

---

### 8. Content Summarizer

Condenses long content into clear, structured key points.

```
Summarize the following content into a structured overview.

Output format:
1. **One-sentence TL;DR** — the single most important thing to understand
2. **Key Points** — 5 bullet points covering the main ideas
3. **Takeaways / Action Items** — what someone should do or remember after reading this
4. **Notable Quotes** — 1-2 direct quotes worth highlighting (if applicable)

Keep the summary objective — reflect the source material accurately, not your opinion.

[PASTE CONTENT HERE]
```

---

### 9. Brainstorm Generator

Rapid-fire idea generation for any topic, product, or problem.

```
Generate a brainstorm for: [TOPIC / PROBLEM / IDEA]

Context: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF WHAT YOU'RE WORKING ON]
Goal: [WHAT OUTCOME ARE YOU TRYING TO ACHIEVE]

Produce 15 distinct ideas. Range from obvious/safe to creative/unconventional.
For each idea, write: the idea in one sentence + one line explaining why it could work.

Format as a numbered list. No grouping or headers — just the raw ideas.
After the list, flag your top 3 picks and briefly explain why.
```

## Image Prompts

### 10. Cinematic Portrait

A richly detailed photorealistic portrait prompt.

```
Close-up portrait of [SUBJECT DESCRIPTION], studio photography, soft diffused
side lighting with a warm golden key light and cool blue fill, shallow depth of field,
skin texture detail, natural expression, [MOOD: serene / intense / contemplative],
shot on a Hasselblad medium format camera, 85mm lens, f/1.8, photorealistic, 8K HDR,
clean dark background with subtle gradient
```

**Customization tips:**

- Replace `[SUBJECT DESCRIPTION]` with demographic, features, clothing, era
- Swap `studio photography` for `golden hour outdoor`, `overcast natural light`, or `candlelight`
- Add `color grade: Kodak Portra 400 film emulation` for a warm analog feel

---

### 11. Fantasy Environment / Concept Art

An atmospheric scene for fantasy, sci-fi, or surreal worlds.

```
[ENVIRONMENT TYPE: ancient library / underwater city / floating island / neon-lit alley],
concept art, wide establishing shot, [TIME OF DAY: dusk / midnight / dawn],
[LIGHTING: god rays filtering through / bioluminescent glow / neon reflections on rain-slicked surface],
color palette: [PALETTE: deep jewel tones / muted earth tones / high-contrast neon],
intricate architectural detail, atmospheric haze, sense of scale and depth,
digital painting in the style of [STUDIO GHIBLI / Craig Mullins / Alphonse Mucha],
ultra-detailed, 4K
```

### 12. Cinematic Video Scene

A motion-ready video prompt with scene, camera, and pacing.

```
[SCENE DESCRIPTION: a lone figure walking through a rain-soaked city street at night],
cinematic videography, [CAMERA MOVEMENT: slow dolly push-in / tracking shot from behind /
overhead drone pullback], [SPEED: real-time / subtle slow motion],
film grain, shallow depth of field, [COLOR GRADE: cold desaturated / warm amber / neon-drenched],
[ATMOSPHERE: fog / rain / dust particles / smoke wisps],
[DURATION: 5-8 seconds], smooth motion, high production value, IMAX quality
```

## Using These Templates

1. **Copy** a template from this page.
2. **Paste** it into Promptzy using the **"+" / Add Prompt** button.
3. **Fill in the brackets** with your specific values, or leave them as placeholders for a reusable template.
4. **Tag it** (e.g., `system`, `creative`, `image`, `client-work`) for quick retrieval later.

<Aside type="tip">
  You can store template *skeletons* (with the brackets intact) in Promptzy as
  starting points. When you need one, copy it, fill in the brackets, and go.
</Aside>
